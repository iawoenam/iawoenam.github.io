About 👋🏾

Building Decarbonization Engineer at CA Air Resources Board🏙️

Ghana-Born 🌍, Baltimore-Raised 🦀, LA-Based 🌴 | he/him

Hi everyone, my name is Awoe (ah-way). I am an urban sustainability professional working towards building climate resilience in our built environment and in our local communities. I graduated from Johns Hopkins University with my BS in Civil Engineering and then Stanford University with my MS in Sustainable Design and Construction. I am passionate about the intersection between the built environment, public health, and social justice. I believe the built environment has the power to strengthen communities by making them more accessible. With that, decades of systematic and structural racism are embedded in policies that enforce the built environment's design around us. These policies accentuate the social determinants that dictate people's well-being in our world. With climate change's hazards upon us, these disparities WILL be further exaggerated, leaving the most vulnerable suffering the most significant burden. I am on a journey to radically revolutionize the world as we know it for a truly equitable and accessible world for all people.

I am also passionate about educating and empowering folks to engage civically and fight for a more just future. I do this through activism and advocacy with Urban Environmentalists LA and Sunrise Movement LA. Finally, I write and podcast with Fostering Our Earth, a space I created to help folks imagine what a sustainable future can really look like. Outside of all of this, you can find me running, playing soccer, supporting Arsenal FC, or searching for the best breakfast burrito in Southern California.
